Project Overview

I work at a partner firm of BMW, supporting the ABK design team for the Navigation App. ABK, short for "Anzeige- und Bedienkomponente" (German for display and control interface), refers to the in-car interface design. Collaborating closely with the design team, developers, project managers, and function owners, we are focused on optimizing the user experience for BMW's in-car navigation system.

Teams and My Role

I work as an interface designer on various BMW projects, focusing on features like parking finder, video AR, event-line, proposals, and destination input. These features are integral to the navigation app for both BMW and Mini brands.

Timeline and Tasks

I am working on Jira tickets, which generally allow 2 weeks time for a tasks. This includes doing requirement analysis together with function owner or project manager, searching existing concepts and user flows, generating optimization ideas, discussing with different stakeholders and going through several reviews.

Tools

Figma was my primary design tool, with Adobe Photoshop and Sketch used based on project requirements. I also utilized FigJam for design workshops and internal heuristic evaluations.

Topic 1: Parking finder

The Function Owner aims to optimize the user flow and interaction for the parking finder feature, including the parking search route and park-out event. He seeks a smooth user experience, clear visualization and explanations of key visual elements, and alignment across different product generations and brands.

Goals and tasks

We aim to optimize the user flow and interaction for the parking finder feature, and seek a smooth user experience, clear visualization and explanations of key visual elements, and alignment across different product generations and brands. My tasks contain:

  • clarifying and prioritizing user stories and needs with the Function Owner
  • creating wireframes and prototypes
  • going through reviews with Product Owners, designers, and Function Owners
  • handing the final version over to the design team

Challenges and approaches

Challenges included: UI element and information overload, diverse use cases, maintaining design consistency, and decision-making within an interdisciplinary team. Approaches I have taken: prioritizing information based on user needs and behavioral patterns in each scenario; incorporating user feedback, usability testing, and analytics to validate decisions; facilitating alignment across generations and brands to create a more cohesive experience.

Results

Parking finder interaction design was optimized and aligned among generation and brands, FO and stakeholders were satisfied and we are still waiting results from usability testing.

Topic 2: Video AR

This AR enhanced navigation feature aims at increasing ease, accuracy, and safety of navigation by adding 3D overlay of visual elements in real-time video.

Goals and tasks

My tasks contain:

  • clarifying and prioritizing user stories and needs with the Function Owner
  • creating elements for ADAS functions
  • building click-dummies for communication
  • going through reviews and handing the final version over to the design team

Challenges and approaches

Challenges included designing intuitive interfaces for complex ADAS functions and ensuring design consistency across various contexts.I spent time analyzing ADAS functions, use cases, and user needs, and used visualization to facilitate communication, idea discussions, and decision-making.

Results

The design process was accelerated, and the design version was optimized.

Topic 3: Other Navi Topics

Working in an Agile environment with Jira tickets across various topics, I became familiar with areas such as destination input, route planning, and proposals. This broadened my understanding of the product and UX as a whole.

Goals and challenges

The process is similar to what was previously described: clarifying and prioritizing user stories and needs with the FO, creating solutions, and conducting reviews with stakeholders.

Challenges and approaches

The main challenge lies in addressing diverse use cases, ensuring consistency across different use cases and generations, while managing multiple topics in parallel. My approaches include reviewing various scenarios, prototyping, employing visualization skills such as persona and journey mapping for communication with cross-functional teams, and searching for relevant data for decision-making.

Results

Requirements have been fullfilled with high satisfaction of stakeholders and improved UX design.
